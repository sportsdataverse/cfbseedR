# `cfb_toy_tiebreakers` ‚Äî official per-conference tiebreaker parity fixture

Designed (not random) fixture exercising the `CONFERENCE_TIEBREAKERS` registry
in `sportsdataverse/cfb/cfb_standings.py`. Shared cross-language oracle: the R
`cfbseedR` port replays `toy_games.csv` + `toy_teams.csv` through the same
engine design and its sorted standings output is diffed against
`expected_standings.csv` (generated by the Python engine,
`tiebreaker_depth="POINTS"`, no `rng` seed needed ‚Äî every tie below resolves
deterministically without reaching the `coin_toss` rung).

## Designed resolution paths

### SEC (`A`, `B`, `C`) ‚Äî resolves at `capped_scoring_margin`

A 3-team round robin, each team 1-1 in conference (tied at `conf_pct=0.5`):

- `A` beats `B` 50-0, `B` beats `C` 20-17, `C` beats `A` 20-17.

Walking the SEC rung list on the full 3-way tie: `h2h` (combined pct 0.5 for
all three ‚Äî no reduction), `record_vs_common` (no common opponent outside the
tied trio ‚Äî no reduction), `record_vs_common_desc` (no conference teams
outside the tied trio to descend through ‚Äî no reduction), `opp_conf_win_pct`
(pooled opponents' conf win pct is symmetric at 0.5 for all three ‚Äî no
reduction), **`capped_scoring_margin`** (42 scored / 48 allowed per game,
summed): `A` = (min(50,42)-min(0,48)) + (min(17,42)-min(20,48)) = 42-3 = 39;
`B` = (min(0,42)-min(50,48)) + (min(20,42)-min(17,48)) = -48+3 = -45;
`C` = (min(17,42)-min(20,48)) + (min(20,42)-min(17,48)) = -3+3 = 0. `A` is the
unique max (39) -> seeded #1 by `capped_scoring_margin`.

Per the registry's restart rule, the remaining pair (`B`, `C`) restarts from
rung 1: `B` beat `C` head-to-head directly (20-17), so `h2h` decides the
second/third split immediately (`B` > `C`) without ever reaching
`capped_scoring_margin` again ‚Äî this is expected and correct: the official
procedure resolves each shrinking tied set independently, it doesn't require
one rung to resolve the *entire* original group.

Expected: `A` (1) > `B` (2) > `C` (3).

### Big 12 (`X`, `Y`, `Z`) ‚Äî resolves at `total_wins` (FCS cap)

`X` and `Y` are both 1-0 in conference (each beat `Z` once; `Z` is 0-2 and
clearly last, not part of the tie). `X` and `Y` never played each other, so
`h2h` skips (no games between them); their only common conference opponent is
`Z`, and both went 1-0 vs `Z` (`record_vs_common` ties); `opp_conf_win_pct`
pooled is identical for both (their only conference opponent is `Z`, 0-2
either way ‚Äî ties). The tie survives to **`total_wins`**, which uses OVERALL
wins with the Big 12's max-one-win-vs-FCS-or-lower cap:

- `X` also beat two additional opponents (`FCS1`, `FCS2`) that are absent from
  `toy_teams.csv` ‚Äî an opponent missing from `teams` is treated as
  FCS-or-lower by convention (see `cfb_standings.py::_standings_base`).
  Raw wins = 3 (`Z`, `FCS1`, `FCS2`); FCS-capped wins = 1 (conf, vs FBS `Z`)
  plus min(2, 1) (capped FCS wins) = **2**.
- `Y` also beat `W` (present in `toy_teams.csv`, `division="FBS"`, a
  single-team `"Other"` conference ‚Äî incidental, not itself under test) and
  `FCS3` (absent from `teams`, so FCS-or-lower by convention). Raw wins = 3
  (`Z`, `W`, `FCS3`); FCS-capped wins = 1 (conf) + 1 (uncapped FBS win vs `W`)
  plus min(1, 1) (capped FCS win) = **3**.

`Y`'s capped total (3) beats `X`'s (2) even though their RAW win totals tie
3-3 ‚Äî the fixture is designed so the FCS cap is the only thing that makes a
difference, proving the cap is actually applied (not a no-op).

Expected: `Y` (1) > `X` (2) > `Z` (3).

## Files

- `toy_games.csv` ‚Äî `sim`/`season` 2024, 9 games (3 SEC round-robin + 6 Big 12
  / cross-conference). `home_points`/`away_points` are populated only for the
  SEC games (the only rows that feed `capped_scoring_margin`); Big 12 rows
  leave them blank (null) ‚Äî harmless since the Big 12 tie never reaches that
  rung.
- `toy_teams.csv` ‚Äî 7 teams (`A`/`B`/`C` SEC, `X`/`Y`/`Z` Big 12, `W` a
  single-team `"Other"` conference for `Y`'s FBS win) with a `division`
  column (`"FBS"` for every listed team ‚Äî `FCS1`/`FCS2`/`FCS3` are
  deliberately NOT listed, to exercise the "unknown opponent counts as
  FCS-or-lower" default).
- `expected_standings.csv` ‚Äî `sim`, `team`, `conference`, `conf_rank`,
  `conf_champ` from `cfb_standings(games, teams, tiebreaker_depth="POINTS")`.
